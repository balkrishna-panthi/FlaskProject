Great Wall of China

A historical marvel stretching over 13,000 miles, one of the most iconic landmarks in China.

Forbidden City

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and former imperial palace located in Beijing.

Terracotta Army

Thousands of life-sized sculptures depicting the army of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China.

Li River

Known for its stunning limestone karst peaks and a popular destination for river cruises in southern China.

Mount Huangshan

Famous for its uniquely shaped granite peaks, pine trees, and sea of clouds.

Summer Palace

A vast imperial garden and palace complex located in Beijing, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Zhangjiajie National Forest Park

Famous for its towering sandstone pillars, which inspired the floating mountains in the movie "Avatar."

Potala Palace

A former residence of the Dalai Lama and a sacred pilgrimage site in Lhasa, Tibet.

West Lake

A scenic freshwater lake in Hangzhou, famous for its picturesque landscapes and cultural significance.

Jiuzhaigou Valley

A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its crystal-clear lakes, waterfalls, and snow-capped mountains.

Yellow Mountain

Known for its steep granite peaks, ancient pine trees, and misty views, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
