Singapore

Singapore, officially the Republic of Singapore, is an island city-state in maritime Southeast Asia, consisting of one main island and 63 additional islands and islets. Located about one degree north of the equator, it is bordered by the Strait of Malacca to the west, the Singapore Strait to the south, the South China Sea to the east, and the Straits of Johor and the State of Johor in Malaysia to the north.

Attractions

Singapore, known as one of the most expensive cities globally, boasts numerous accolades such as being the cleanest and best planned, along with being exceptionally entertaining. The Lion City features attractions like the largest rooftop infinity pool, the world's first nocturnal safari park, centuries-old temples, stunning gardens, impressive architecture, and a vibrant food scene, creating a captivating blend of history and modernity.

Cuisine

Singapore, despite its small size, is a gastronomic powerhouse, showcasing a vibrant dining scene influenced by its rich cultural diversity stemming from 19th and 20th-century immigration. This culinary tapestry is prominently displayed at hawker centres, where a variety of affordable dishes reflect the unique traditions of Chinese, Malay, and Indian cuisines.
