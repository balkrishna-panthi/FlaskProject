Sigiriya Rock Fortress

An ancient rock fortress and one of the most iconic landmarks in Sri Lanka, featuring stunning frescoes and panoramic views.

Temple of the Tooth

A sacred Buddhist temple in Kandy, home to a relic of the Buddha's tooth, attracting pilgrims and visitors from all over the world.

Yala National Park

Famous for its wildlife and leopards, Yala is Sri Lanka’s most visited national park, with diverse ecosystems and a rich variety of animals.

Ella Rock

A beautiful trekking destination offering breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys, tea plantations, and mountains.

Galle Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage site with a rich history, Galle Fort offers stunning views of the ocean and charming streets lined with colonial architecture.

Nuwara Eliya

Known as 'Little England,' Nuwara Eliya is a hill station with cool weather, tea plantations, and charming British colonial-era buildings.

Anuradhapura

A UNESCO World Heritage city, Anuradhapura is home to ancient ruins, stupas, and temples that reflect Sri Lanka’s rich Buddhist history.

Mirissa Beach

A stunning beach destination known for its golden sands, crystal-clear waters, and the chance to see whales and dolphins.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

A unique experience to interact with and observe rescued elephants, one of the largest herds in captivity in the world.

Udawalawe National Park

A wildlife haven where you can spot elephants, leopards, and various other species in a natural, untouched environment.
