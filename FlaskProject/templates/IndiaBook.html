Taj Mahal

A world-famous monument and symbol of love, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

Qutub Minar

A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture.

Red Fort

An iconic symbol of India's history, the Red Fort in Delhi was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for around 200 years.

Jaipur City Palace

A royal palace complex in Jaipur, the City Palace is known for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and vibrant courtyards.

Gateway of India

A monumental archway built during the British Raj in Mumbai, the Gateway of India symbolizes the triumph of India’s independence.

Kerala Backwaters

A network of tranquil lagoons and rivers, Kerala’s backwaters offer a serene escape, best experienced through houseboat cruises.

Meenakshi Temple

Located in Madurai, Meenakshi Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves

Ancient rock-cut caves featuring intricate sculptures and paintings, these caves are UNESCO World Heritage sites, representing Indian culture and heritage.

The Himalayas

The Himalayas offer breathtaking views and are a paradise for trekkers and nature lovers, with some of the highest peaks in the world.

Khajuraho Temples

Famous for their stunning sculptures and intricate carvings, the Khajuraho Temples are a UNESCO World Heritage site showcasing India's rich art and culture.
